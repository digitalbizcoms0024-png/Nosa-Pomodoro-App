Pomofocus Alternatives: 5 Better Pomodoro Timers for 2026

February 22, 2026 9 min read

Pomofocus has been a go-to Pomodoro timer for years, and for good reason. It is simple, free, and gets the job done for basic time-boxing. But if you have been using it for a while, you have probably bumped into its limits. Maybe the lack of offline support has burned you during a flight or a Wi-Fi outage. Maybe you have ADHD and 25-minute blocks feel completely arbitrary. Or maybe you just want more than a countdown and a ding.

You are not alone. Thousands of people search for Pomofocus alternatives every month because they have outgrown its simplicity. And in 2026, the landscape of Pomodoro timer apps has matured considerably. There are tools that adapt to how your brain actually works, not just how Francesco Cirillo's original technique prescribed.

In this guide, we compare the five best Pomodoro timer alternatives available right now, examining each one's strengths, weaknesses, and who it is best suited for. Whether you need the best free Pomodoro timer for ADHD, a gamified experience, or deep Apple ecosystem integration, there is an option here for you.

Why People Look for Pomofocus Alternatives

Before we dive into the alternatives, it helps to understand what specifically drives people away from Pomofocus. These are not minor quibbles. They are real workflow blockers that affect daily productivity:

  • No offline support. Pomofocus is a web app with no service worker or Progressive Web App (PWA) capabilities. If your internet drops, your timer disappears. For remote workers, travelers, and anyone in a spotty-Wi-Fi environment, this is a dealbreaker.
  • No ADHD-specific features. The rigid 25/5/15 structure does not account for hyperfocus, time blindness, or the need for flexible interval lengths. ADHD brains often need shorter sprints (15 minutes) or longer hyperfocus sessions (45+ minutes), and Pomofocus gives you limited control.
  • Basic visual design. The interface has not evolved significantly. There is no visual progress indicator beyond a simple countdown, which makes it hard to feel a sense of momentum or progress through your work sessions.
  • No analytics or tracking. You cannot see how many hours you focused last week, what your streak looks like, or whether your productivity is improving. Without data, you are flying blind.
  • No installable app experience. You cannot add it to your dock or home screen as a standalone app. It always lives in a browser tab, competing with fifty other tabs for your attention.

Now let us look at the tools that solve these problems.

What makes it different is the philosophy behind it. Rather than forcing every user into the same rigid 25-minute box, Pomodoro Timer offers flexible interval presets. You can run 15-minute sprints for high-distraction days, standard 25-minute blocks for routine work, or 45-minute deep work sessions when you are locked in. This kind of flexibility is not a nice-to-have for ADHD users. It is essential.

  • Dark mode. A proper dark theme that is easy on the eyes during late-night work sessions. The mode switches between focus and break states with distinct visual cues so you always know what phase you are in.
  • Task list integration. Attach tasks to your Pomodoro sessions so you are never staring at a timer wondering what you should be working on.
  • Completely free. No ads, no freemium walls for core features. The timer, presets, analytics, and offline mode are all available at no cost.

2. Forest — Best for Gamification

Forest takes a completely different approach to Pomodoro timing by turning your focus sessions into a game. When you start a timer, you plant a virtual tree. If you leave the app before the timer ends, your tree dies. Over time, you grow a forest that visually represents your productivity history.

The concept is clever, and it works surprisingly well for people motivated by visual rewards and streaks. Forest has also partnered with tree-planting organizations, so your virtual trees can translate into real trees planted around the world. That feel-good factor gives you an extra nudge to stay focused.

Key Features

  • Gamified focus sessions. Plant trees, grow a forest, and unlock new species as you accumulate focus time. The visual garden serves as both a progress tracker and a motivational tool.
  • Real tree planting. Spend virtual coins (earned through focus) to plant real trees through the Trees for the Future organization.
  • Friends feature. Plant trees together with friends or coworkers for accountability. If anyone in the group leaves the app, the shared tree dies.
  • Blocklist. On mobile, Forest can block distracting apps during focus sessions, adding an extra layer of discipline.

Pros

  • Highly motivating gamification system
  • Real-world impact through tree planting
  • Social accountability features
  • Available on iOS, Android, and as a Chrome extension

Cons

  • Paid app ($3.99 on iOS, free with ads on Android)
  • No ADHD-specific features or flexible intervals
  • The gamification can feel gimmicky once the novelty wears off
  • No detailed analytics or focus trend data
  • No web app for desktop (only the Chrome extension, which is limited)

Best For

People who respond well to gamification and visual rewards. Great for students and anyone who needs an extra motivation layer beyond the timer itself. Less ideal for ADHD users who need interval flexibility.

3. Focus Keeper — Best iOS Experience

Focus Keeper is a polished, well-designed Pomodoro timer built specifically for iOS. If you live on your iPhone and want a native app experience with smooth animations and reliable notifications, Focus Keeper delivers. It has been around since the early days of the App Store and has refined its interface over many iterations.

The app supports customizable work and break intervals, which gives it an edge over Pomofocus for people who want to tweak their session lengths. It also includes a nice daily goal system where you set a target number of Pomodoros and track your progress with a visual chart.

Key Features

  • Customizable intervals. Adjust focus time from 1 to 99 minutes, with separate break and long break controls. More flexibility than Pomofocus, though still a manual configuration rather than smart presets.
  • Daily goal tracking. Set a target number of sessions per day and watch your progress fill in. Historical charts show your productivity over days and weeks.
  • Sound and vibration options. Choose from multiple timer sounds, ticking options, and haptic feedback. The sensory customization is genuinely useful for maintaining focus awareness.
  • Clean native design. Focus Keeper looks and feels like a first-class iOS app, not a web wrapper. Animations are smooth, and the interface respects iOS design conventions.

Pros

  • Beautiful, native iOS design
  • Customizable work and break intervals
  • Daily goal tracking with visual charts
  • Reliable notifications even when the app is backgrounded

Cons

  • iOS only. No Android, no web version, no desktop app
  • Free version has ads; Pro is $3.99
  • No offline web option. Requires the App Store
  • No ADHD-specific features or preset-based workflows
  • No task management integration

Best For

iPhone users who want a polished, reliable Pomodoro app and do not need cross-platform support. If your entire workflow happens on iOS, Focus Keeper is a solid choice.

4. Marinara Timer — Best for Simplicity and Teams

Marinara Timer strips the Pomodoro concept down to its absolute essence. It is a free web-based timer with no account required, no downloads, and no setup. You open the page, pick a timer type, and start. That is it.

What makes Marinara unique is its shareable timer feature. You can create a timer and share the URL with your team, and everyone sees the same countdown in real time. This makes it useful for pair programming, study groups, or remote teams who want synchronized focus sessions without installing any software.

Key Features

  • Three timer types. Choose between Pomodoro (work/break cycles), Custom (set your own intervals), and Kitchen (a simple countdown). The Pomodoro mode follows the classic 25/5/15 structure.
  • Shareable timer links. Generate a unique URL for your timer that anyone can join. All participants see the same countdown, making it easy to coordinate group focus sessions.
  • Zero setup. No account creation, no downloads, no configuration. Open the page and start the timer. This is about as frictionless as it gets.
  • Browser notifications. Get notified when a session ends, even if the tab is in the background. Works in any modern browser.

Pros

  • Completely free with no ads
  • Shareable timers for team synchronization
  • Zero friction. No account or setup required
  • Works in any browser on any platform

Cons

  • Very minimal feature set. No analytics, no task list, no progress tracking
  • No offline support
  • No visual progress indicator
  • No ADHD-friendly features or flexible presets
  • Interface is functional but dated
  • No installable app or PWA

Best For

Teams that need a quick, shareable timer with no friction. Also good as a backup timer when you just need something simple and fast. Not ideal for individual productivity tracking or ADHD users.

5. Be Focused — Best for the Apple Ecosystem

Be Focused (also known as Be Focused Pro) is a Pomodoro timer designed for the Apple ecosystem. It runs on Mac, iPhone, and iPad, syncing your data across devices via iCloud. If you are already invested in Apple's platform and want your Pomodoro data to follow you from your desk to your phone, Be Focused handles that seamlessly.

The standout feature is its task integration. You can create a list of tasks, estimate how many Pomodoros each one will take, and then work through them sequentially. The app tracks actual vs. estimated Pomodoros, which over time helps you get better at estimating how long things take. This is a valuable skill for anyone, but especially useful for people who struggle with time perception.

Key Features

  • Task-based workflow. Create tasks, assign estimated Pomodoro counts, and track actual time spent. This bridges the gap between a timer and a lightweight project management tool.
  • Cross-device sync. iCloud sync keeps your tasks, settings, and history consistent across your Mac, iPhone, and iPad.
  • Customizable intervals. Adjust work intervals from 1 to 120 minutes, breaks from 1 to 60 minutes, and set your long break interval. Reasonable flexibility for most users.
  • Mac menu bar integration. On macOS, Be Focused lives in your menu bar for quick access without switching windows. Start and stop timers without disrupting your flow.
  • Reporting. View your completed Pomodoros by day, week, or custom date range. Export data as CSV for further analysis.

Pros

  • Excellent Apple ecosystem integration with iCloud sync
  • Task estimation helps improve time perception
  • Mac menu bar widget is genuinely convenient
  • CSV export for data portability

Cons

  • Apple only. No Windows, no Android, no web version
  • Free version is limited; Pro is $4.99
  • No ADHD-specific features or adaptive intervals
  • Interface design feels slightly outdated compared to modern alternatives
  • No visual progress ring or spatial time representation

Best For

Apple users who work across multiple devices and want their Pomodoro data synced automatically. The task estimation feature is a genuine differentiator for anyone who needs to improve their time awareness.

Comparison Summary

Here is how all five Pomofocus alternatives stack up across the features that matter most:

Offline Support: Only Pomodoro Timer (PWA) and the native apps (Forest, Focus Keeper, Be Focused) work offline. Marinara Timer requires an internet connection, just like Pomofocus.

ADHD-Friendly Features: Pomodoro Timer is the only option specifically designed with ADHD users in mind, offering flexible presets and a visual progress ring that combats time blindness. The others offer customizable intervals but lack ADHD-specific design thinking.

Price: Pomodoro Timer and Marinara Timer are completely free. Forest, Focus Keeper, and Be Focused all have paid versions or in-app purchases to unlock full functionality.

Platform Availability: Pomodoro Timer and Marinara Timer work on any platform via the web. Forest covers iOS, Android, and Chrome. Focus Keeper is iOS-only. Be Focused is Apple-only. If cross-platform access matters to you, the web-based options win.

Analytics: Pomodoro Timer and Be Focused offer the best focus tracking and analytics. Forest shows your tree history but lacks depth. Focus Keeper has daily charts. Marinara Timer has no analytics at all.

Visual Progress: Pomodoro Timer's SVG progress ring is the most visually engaging progress indicator in this group. Forest's growing tree is charming but abstract. The others rely on simple countdowns.

Team Features: Marinara Timer is the clear winner for teams with its shareable timer links. Forest has a friends feature. The others are individual-focused tools.

Which Pomofocus Alternative Should You Choose?

The right tool depends on what specifically is not working for you with Pomofocus. Here is a quick decision framework:

  • If you have ADHD or time blindness, use Pomodoro Timer. The flexible intervals, visual progress ring, and ADHD-aware design make it the strongest option for neurodivergent users.
  • If you need gamification to stay motivated, try Forest. The tree-planting mechanic is genuinely clever and the social pressure of shared sessions adds accountability.
  • If you are iPhone-only and want polish, Focus Keeper is your best bet. It is the most refined native iOS Pomodoro app available.
  • If you need a team timer right now with no setup, Marinara Timer cannot be beat for speed and simplicity.
  • If you are deep in the Apple ecosystem, Be Focused's iCloud sync and menu bar integration make it the natural choice for Mac and iOS users.

For most people searching for Pomofocus alternatives in 2026, Pomodoro Timer hits the best balance of features, design, and price. It solves the core complaints people have with Pomofocus: no offline mode, no visual progress, no analytics, and no flexibility for different work styles. And it does it all for free, in a clean interface that respects your attention rather than fighting for it.

Whatever you choose, the most important thing is to actually use the technique. A Pomodoro timer only works if you press start. Pick the tool that lowers that barrier for you, and commit to at least one focused session today.
